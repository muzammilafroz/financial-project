## Suggested Literature Review Argument

The literature should be organised around four claims. First, growth should be analysed as a sequence of regimes rather than a single average, following the growth-accelerations and structural-break literature. Second, India's own growth transition is contested: some evidence places the shift before 1991, while other work cautions against attributing growth acceleration mechanically to liberalisation. Third, Indian states do not share one convergence path; the literature repeatedly finds divergence, polarisation, club convergence, and infrastructure-linked heterogeneity. Fourth, inflation and COVID shocks have strong regional components, especially through food supply chains, fiscal capacity, mobility restrictions, and spatially uneven demand and supply conditions. This makes Arunachal Pradesh a defensible case for comparing a frontier hill state with all India, Assam, Sikkim, Himachal Pradesh, Tripura, and Meghalaya.
